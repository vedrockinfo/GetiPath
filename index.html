Different types of AI

  • Narrow or Weak AI (ANI)
    • Designed to perform specific tasks or solve particular problems.
    • Lacks general intelligence and cannot perform tasks outside its scope.
    • Example include virtual personal assistants (ex- Siri, Alexa), recommendation systems(eg- Netflix recommendations) and Chatbots
    • General AI (AGI)
    • Also known as Strong Al or AGI (Artificial General Intelligence).
    • Possesses human-like cognitive abilities and can understand, learn, and perform any intellectual task that a human can.
    • Currently, true AGI does not exist, and creating it remains a challenging goal in Al research.
  • Artificial Superintelligence (ASI)
    • A hypothetical level of Al that surpasses human intelligence in all aspects.
    • ASI would have the ability to perform tasks at a superhuman level and potentially make decisions beyond human comprehension.
    • This is a topic of speculation and debate in the field of Al ethics and futurology.
  • Machine Learning (ML)
    • A subset of Al that focuses on developing algorithms and models that enable machines to learn from data and improve their performance over time.
    • Common ML techniques include supervised learning, unsupervised leaming, and reinforcement learning.
  • Deep Learning (DL)
    • A specialized subset of machine learning that uses artificial neural networks inspired by the human brain.
    • Deep learning excels in tasks such as image and speech recognition, natural language processing, and playing complex games (e.g., AlphaGo).
  • Reinforcement Learning
    • Reinforcement learning is a machine learning paradigm where an agent learns to make decisions by interacting with an environment and receiving feedback in the form of rewards or punishments.
    • Common applications include game-playing agents and autonomous control systems.
  • Robotics
    • Al in robotics involves the development of intelligent machines that can perform physical tasks in the real world.
    • Examples include industrial robots, autonomous drones, and self-driving cars.
  • Computer Vision
    • Computer vision is a branch of Al that focuses on enabling machines to interpret and understand visual information from the world, such as images and videos.
    • Applications include facial recognition, object detection, and autonomous vehicles
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP)
    • A field of Al that focuses on enabling machines to understand, interpret, and generate human language.
    • NLP is used in applications such as chatbo% language translation, sentiment analysis, and text summarization.
  • Autonomous Al
    • Autonomous Al refers to Al systems that can operate independently without human intervention.
    • Examples include autonomous drones, self-driving cars, and automated industrial processes.
  • Generative AI
    • Text Generation
    • Image Generation
    • Music Generation
    • Video Generation

How does AI works?

Artificial Intelligence (Al) is a broad field of computer science that focuses on creating systems and machines capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence. Al systems work through a combination of data, algorithms, and computational power.
